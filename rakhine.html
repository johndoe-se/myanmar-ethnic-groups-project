Rakhine

The Rakhine people live mainly along Myanmar’s western coast, historically known for the ancient kingdom of Mrauk U and their distinct coastal culture.

  • Traditional Dress

Culture and Traditions

Rakhine culture blends coastal traditions with strong Buddhist influence. The ancient city of Mrauk U was once a major cultural and trading hub.

Rakhine festivals often celebrate the sea and Buddhist religious events, with unique foods such as spicy fish dishes central to their cuisine.

Traditional Dress

Rakhine men wear longyi with checked or striped patterns, while women’s attire features bold, bright colors with simple yet elegant blouses.

Their traditional clothing reflects both coastal practicality and festive vibrancy, often worn during religious and community ceremonies.
