Kayah

The Kayah (Karenni) people live mainly in Kayah State and are known for their distinct traditions and unique cultural practices.

  • Traditional Dress

Culture and Traditions

The Kayah, also called Karenni, maintain a strong identity rooted in animist beliefs, community festivals, and storytelling traditions.

They are widely known for their symbolic totem poles, vibrant dances, and communal way of life, reflecting their deep connection to land and spirit.

Traditional Dress

Kayah traditional dress often features bright red woven tunics, decorated with beads and simple geometric patterns.

Women may wear long tunics with white or red accents, while men wear short tunics paired with handwoven sashes.
