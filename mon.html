Mon

The Mon people are among the earliest civilizations in Southeast Asia, known for spreading Theravada Buddhism and their unique cultural heritage.

Culture and Traditions

The Mon are credited with introducing Theravada Buddhism to Myanmar and Thailand. Their ancient kingdoms, such as Thaton, played a central role in the region’s history.

Mon festivals, literature, and temple architecture show a deep connection to Buddhist traditions and artistic expression.

Traditional Dress

Mon traditional attire is elegant and colorful. Men wear longyi with simple shirts, while women wear beautifully designed longyi with intricate blouses.

Red and earth-tone colors are often used, symbolizing prosperity and tradition, while Mon women’s dress is known for its graceful patterns.
